## Writing Preferences

### Journal Fit

- Every GRL paper must have a clear, impactful take-home message.
- Focus on high-impact, concise research letters across all geosciences.
- The work should represent a significant advance with broad interest.
- Keep the narrative tight: state the problem, present the evidence, deliver the conclusion.

### Style Preferences

- MUST be concise. Every sentence counts. No filler.
- Lead with the key finding; build context quickly.
- Use precise, quantitative claims. Avoid hedging that dilutes the message, but do not overclaim.
- Past tense for methods and results; present tense for established knowledge.
- Figures should be self-contained: clear captions, proper units, minimal redundancy with text.

### Avoid

- Verbose introductions or extensive literature reviews
- Speculative claims without quantitative support
- `novel`, `groundbreaking`, `revolutionary`, or similar self-promotional adjectives
- Detailed methodology that belongs in a methods paper or companion paper
- Discussion that wanders without a clear message
- Redundant text that repeats figure captions

## Quality Checks

### Before Submission

- [ ] Total word count is within the ~4,000 word limit (including references and captions).
- [ ] The abstract is ~150 words with a clear take-home message.
- [ ] The manuscript has a single, clear, impactful conclusion.
- [ ] Figures (3-5) are each high-impact and self-contained.
- [ ] Every section is essential; no filler paragraphs.
- [ ] A data availability statement compliant with AGU data policy is present.
- [ ] Author contributions are included.

### Tense and Tone Checks

- Methods should be extremely brief, usually past tense.
- Results are reported in past tense or present-simple summary form.
- Discussion is minimal; focus on implications and significance.
- Conclusion is direct: state the finding and its importance.

## Section Guidance

### Abstract
- **Purpose:** Deliver the key finding and its significance in ~150 words.
- **Expected content:** Brief context, what was done, key result, why it matters.
- **Common pitfalls:** Too much background, no clear result, vague significance, exceeding 200 words.

### Introduction
- **Purpose:** Quickly establish context, gap, and contribution in ~2 paragraphs.
- **Expected content:** Concise context, the specific gap, what this letter addresses.
- **Common pitfalls:** Literature review too long, gap not clearly identified, delayed contribution statement.

### Methods
- **Purpose:** Provide just enough detail for the reader to evaluate the approach.
- **Expected content:** Essential methodology, data sources, key analytical choices.
- **Common pitfalls:** Too detailed for a letter, missing critical information, unnecessary step-by-step description.

### Results
- **Purpose:** Present findings directly and concisely.
- **Expected content:** Key quantitative results with minimal interpretation, figure references.
- **Common pitfalls:** Too verbose, repeating figure captions, presenting secondary findings.

### Discussion and Conclusions
- **Purpose:** Brief interpretation and statement of significance.
- **Expected content:** Physical or mechanistic interpretation, broader implications, concise conclusion.
- **Common pitfalls:** Extended discussion inappropriate for a letter, repeating results, vague implications.
