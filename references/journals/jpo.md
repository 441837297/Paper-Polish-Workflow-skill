## Writing Preferences

### Journal Fit

- Keep a clear physical oceanography perspective throughout the paper.
- Emphasize quantitative analysis, process-oriented reasoning, and physics-based interpretation.
- Show relevance to ocean dynamics, air-sea interaction, ocean circulation, waves, or mixing.
- Prefer rigorous dynamical explanations over descriptive summaries.

### Style Preferences

- Prefer precise, quantitative claims supported by measurements or model output.
- Use cautious verbs such as `suggest`, `indicate`, or `demonstrate` unless strong causal evidence is available.
- Past tense for methods and results; present tense for general knowledge and established theory.
- Make transition sentences functional and logically connect physical mechanisms.
- Include proper units, coordinates (lat/lon or distance), and error estimates throughout.

### Avoid

- Speculative claims without quantitative support
- Overgeneralization beyond the study region without justification
- `novel`, `groundbreaking`, `revolutionary`, or similar self-promotional adjectives
- Discussion paragraphs that repeat results without mechanistic interpretation
- Vague references to "previous studies" without specific citations

## Quality Checks

### Before Submission

- [ ] The abstract fits the JPO length constraint (~250 words).
- [ ] Data quality control procedures are documented.
- [ ] Statistical methods are appropriate and clearly described.
- [ ] Figures have proper units and coordinates (lat/lon or distance).
- [ ] Physical mechanisms are discussed, not merely described.
- [ ] The manuscript maintains a physical oceanography angle throughout.
- [ ] A data availability statement is present.
- [ ] Author contributions are included.

### Tense and Tone Checks

- Methods are usually described in the past tense.
- Results are usually reported in the past tense.
- Physical interpretation and discussion can shift to present tense when describing established dynamics.
- Conclusion should stay measured; avoid claiming universal validity beyond the study domain.

## Section Guidance

### Abstract
- **Purpose:** State the research problem, approach, key quantitative findings, and physical interpretation compactly.
- **Expected content:** Oceanographic context, gap, data/method, core quantitative result, physical interpretation.
- **Common pitfalls:** Too much background, missing quantitative evidence, vague physical interpretation, no dynamical insight.

### Introduction
- **Purpose:** Establish the oceanographic context, identify the knowledge gap, and state the contribution.
- **Expected content:** Physical oceanography motivation, literature review identifying the gap, specific contribution, paper roadmap.
- **Common pitfalls:** Generic ocean framing without specific dynamical question, weak gap identification, contribution stated too late.

### Data and Methods
- **Purpose:** Describe observation platforms, numerical models, processing methods, and quality control clearly enough for evaluation.
- **Expected content:** Observation platforms (moorings, Argo, satellite, gliders, ship-based, etc.), numerical models, processing methods, quality control procedures, statistical approaches.
- **Common pitfalls:** Missing quality control documentation, insufficient model configuration details, unclear data processing steps.

### Results
- **Purpose:** Present quantitative findings with physical interpretation and spatial/temporal patterns.
- **Expected content:** Main quantitative findings, spatial and temporal patterns, physical interpretation of observed features, figure/table references with proper coordinates and units.
- **Common pitfalls:** Reporting values without interpretation, overclaiming without statistical support, hiding negative or inconclusive findings.

### Discussion
- **Purpose:** Interpret physical mechanisms, compare with prior studies, and address broader implications.
- **Expected content:** Mechanism-level interpretation, comparison to prior oceanographic studies, broader dynamical implications, limitations.
- **Common pitfalls:** Repeating results without mechanistic insight, overstating generalizability, skipping physical explanation.

### Conclusion
- **Purpose:** Close with the main takeaways, bounded contribution, and realistic future directions.
- **Expected content:** Short recap, key quantitative findings, significance, future work.
- **Common pitfalls:** Inflated claims beyond study domain, introducing new evidence, overly broad statements without quantitative support.
